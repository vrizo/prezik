Training sites

Sites the training loop may run against. Only public sites or products we own. Never modify a target, only browse it; forms may only be filled on products we own (pain-tracker). Each site has a golden checklist — the specific things a good demo of THAT site must show. The judge scores coverage against the checklist (reported as hits, e.g. 4/5 in the scoreboard), which keeps "coverage" a diff, not a vibe.

1. https://pain-tracker.app — ours, a gated app: the real product is behind sign-in.
   Credentials: login with vitalii.rizo+test1806@gmail.com / eCZ-UWb-Lrp (test account), or signup mode with @berlin.dog emails when a fresh-account flow is being tested.
   Checklist:
   - creates a pain entry end to end (the core workflow) and shows the saved result
   - shows the pain history/overview after at least one entry exists
   - shows tracking dimensions (intensity/location/notes or equivalent visible fields)
   - narration says who it is for (people tracking chronic pain), matching the brief
   - no sign-in/signup form ever on camera

2. https://closeenough.ai/en — public landing only, no account possible. The demo must present the project from the landing: what it is, the idea, why it is interesting.
   Checklist:
   - opens on the hero and states what the product does in plain words
   - shows the main feature sections with highlights/zooms, not a scroll-through
   - covers pricing or how to get started, if present on the site
   - narration only claims what the landing actually shows
   - feels like a pitch of the product, not a website tour ("here is the pricing" is a tour; "plans start at X" is a pitch)

3. https://www.meetjamie.ai/en — public landing only, no account possible. Same task as closeenough: present the product and the idea from public pages.
   Checklist:
   - states the core value (AI meeting notes) within the first two scenes
   - demonstrates or shows at least two concrete features (e.g. summaries, transcription, privacy angle)
   - shows platform/compatibility or pricing information, if mapped
   - narration only claims what the pages actually show
   - no docs/blog/legal pages on camera

4. https://www.kleinanzeigen.de/stadt/berlin/ — public German classifieds site; no account. The SITE is in German, the SCRIPT must be in English: the demo presents the website, the project, the idea to an English-speaking viewer.
   Checklist:
   - narration is entirely in English while the on-screen UI is German
   - explains what the site is (classifieds marketplace) and the Berlin local angle
   - shows browsing at least one real category or listing area
   - shows search or filtering if the mapped elements allow it
   - handles the cookie banner off camera (never visible in a frame)

Add more sites before overnight training — one per product category (gated SaaS, landing-only product, content/marketplace site, non-English site) so "two different sites" in the promotion rule means genuinely different failure modes.
